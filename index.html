My geographic trail: Ohio→ Boston→ Minnesota→ Boston→ Redmond→ Seattle→ Poulsbo. I worked as a software tester back in the 90s, then moved to a more rural area (Kitsap County). My most recent job was doing the audio recording for a documentary about education around the world (yes, I was the person holding a long pole with a big, fuzzy microphone at the end). While that afforded me the opportunity to see a number of interesting places (Korea, Chile, Sweden and more), I didn't enjoy the actual work as much as I remember enjoying coding.

Over the summer, I dabbled with some online tutorials on my own. I researched a handful of coding boot camps, and - after visiting Galvanize's Pioneer Square location for a free evening class - decided that this was the path I wanted to take.

When I'm not working, I like to garden (though I have trouble finding the time to do so). I've volunteered at Heronswood quite a bit in the past few years, and hope to somehow still be able to find the time to do so while I'm at Galvanize. I love trying-out IPAs and finding new restaurants with good vegetarian food.

I have recently taken-up rock climbing (in the gym - no dangling off of rock faces for me, thank you very much). I'm really enjoying that and have been to both Island Rock on Bainbridge, and Vertical World in Ballard.

Nothing is really work unless you would rather be doing something else. - James M. Barrie"
